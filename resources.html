📰 Many of these papers are behind paywalls — here's how to access them Tips

Most major newspapers now charge for online access. The good news: there are several free and legitimate ways to read them.

1. Library subscriptions — best & most reliable

  • PressReader — free access to thousands of daily newspapers and magazines worldwide. Sign in with your library card details at pressreader.com.
  • Historical archives — many libraries offer free digital access to databases like NewsBank or ProQuest. Check your local library website.

2. Search engine trick

  • Copy the exact headline of the paywalled article. Search for it on Google. Clicking the link directly from search results often bypasses the paywall.

3. Archive sites

  • Internet Archive (Wayback Machine) — paste the URL of a paywalled article into web.archive.org to find a previously saved snapshot.

4. Direct support

  • If you read a paper regularly, consider subscribing. Many publications offer discounted introductory rates or "digital only" tiers at a fraction of the print price. Supporting independent journalism keeps it independent.
