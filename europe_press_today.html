🔭 What Germany's Press Sees That The World Misses

The industrial hollowing-out
Germany is quietly deindustrialising — factories moving to the US, Eastern Europe, or closing. This is the biggest structural story in European economics and almost no foreign press covers it with the urgency German papers do.
The AfD cordon sanitaire
How long can every other German party refuse to govern with the AfD when it represents 20%+ of voters? The constitutional tension here is enormous and barely understood abroad.
East vs West Germany
35 years after reunification the economic, cultural and political gap between Ost and West remains stark. The AfD's strength in the East is a symptom. The disease is still being debated.
German memory culture
Debates about Holocaust remembrance, Erinnerungskultur, and what Germany "owes" are constant and intense domestically. Internationally this is seen as settled. It isn't.

🔭 What Italy's Press Sees That The World Misses

The Mezzogiorno as Europe's forgotten crisis
Southern Italy's youth unemployment, emigration, and economic stagnation is a chronic EU failure story that gets almost no international coverage. It's as large a structural problem as anything in Greece.
Meloni as a pragmatist
The international image of Meloni as a dangerous far-right leader doesn't match her actual governing record. Italian centre papers cover a far more complicated picture. This gap between image and reality is a blind spot in foreign reporting.
The RAI culture war
Italy's public broadcaster has become a proxy battleground for control of national culture. This is treated as domestic trivia abroad. Italians understand it as fundamental to democratic health.
Organised crime's business model
The Mafia (and 'Ndrangheta, Camorra) remain major actors in the Italian and European economy. Italian papers cover this seriously and regularly. International media treats it as almost picturesque.

🔭 What Spain's Press Sees That The World Misses

Catalan independence — still unresolved
The world moved on from the 2017 independence crisis. Spanish papers never did. The constitutional status of Catalonia remains genuinely unresolved and shapes every coalition negotiation in Madrid.
Spain's housing emergency
Spain's rental crisis is worse than France or Germany's on several metrics, but receives a fraction of the international coverage. The under-35s' inability to leave home is reshaping society — foreign press has barely noticed.
The water wars
Spain's internal politics around water allocation — between Valencia, Murcia, and Castile — are becoming a flashpoint as climate change reduces supply. A preview of what much of southern Europe faces. Ignored internationally.
Morocco as Spain's existential neighbour
Spain's relationship with Morocco — over migration, Ceuta and Melilla, fisheries, and Saharan sovereignty — is more complex and consequential than most Europeans realise. Spanish papers treat it as a top foreign policy concern. Abroad it's barely a footnote.

🔭 What Poland's Press Sees That The World Misses

Poland's existential Russia anxiety
No Western European paper covers Ukraine with Poland's raw fear. For Poland, Russia's war isn't a geopolitical story — it's a personal memory of invasion, occupation, and genocide. The emotional register is entirely different.
Poland's democratic recovery
The reversal of PiS-era judicial capture is one of the most important stories in European democratic history. The rest of Europe has largely stopped paying attention. Poland lives it daily.
Poland's economic miracle
Poland has had the fastest sustained economic growth of any large EU member for three decades. This is barely covered internationally, which still mostly stereotypes Poland through a post-communist lens.
The abortion debate's constitutional dimension
Poland's abortion impasse isn't just a political story — it's a constitutional crisis about what courts can do, what coalition politics allows, and whether elected majorities can override entrenched judicial decisions. Far more complex than international coverage suggests.
