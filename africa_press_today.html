🇬🇭 Ghana — Press Today
◀ Left / Progressive
The Chronicle
Independent daily. Investigative focus, critical of power — government or opposition alike. Strong voice for civil society.
Youth unemployment at 47%: Mahama's first 100 days must deliver more than rhetoric ▼
Ghana's youth unemployment crisis — nearly half of under-25s without formal work — is The Chronicle's defining domestic issue. The paper argues the new NDC government won the December election on a change mandate but must now translate campaign promises into visible economic relief within its first year, or face the same disillusionment that ended the NPP's tenure. → thechronicle.com.gh
Galamsey crisis: illegal gold mining still destroying Pra and Offin rivers despite crackdowns ▼
Illegal small-scale mining (galamsey) has contaminated major rivers and destroyed farmland across Ghana's forest belt. The Chronicle has consistently covered this as an environmental justice issue — disproportionately harming rural communities while profits flow to connected networks. The new government has pledged renewed enforcement but The Chronicle is sceptical given the issue's deep political entanglement. → thechronicle.com.gh
IMF programme conditions: are austerity measures punishing the poor to rescue the cedi? ▼
Ghana secured an IMF bailout in 2023 after a debt default. The Chronicle questions whether the conditions — public sector wage restraint, subsidy cuts, utility price increases — are exacting too high a social cost. It argues the IMF programme stabilises macro numbers while deepening everyday poverty, and calls on the Mahama government to negotiate softer conditionalities. → thechronicle.com.gh

● Centre
Daily Graphic
Ghana's largest-circulation daily. State-founded but editorially centrist. Record of covering government and opposition with relative balance.
Mahama meets investors: government seeks $2bn infrastructure push to kickstart growth ▼
President Mahama has held a series of investor roadshows in Accra and Abidjan, pitching Ghana's post-stabilisation credentials to foreign capital. Daily Graphic reports the government is targeting roads, ports, and digital infrastructure as the primary sectors, with a $2bn pipeline across three years. Analysts quoted are cautiously optimistic but note that political risk perception remains elevated following the 2022 default. → graphic.com.gh
Cocoa season: COCOBOD sets farmgate price up 50% but farmers say it still isn't enough ▼
Ghana's cocoa farmers received a 50% price increase from the Ghana Cocoa Board (COCOBOD) this season — but with global cocoa prices at historic highs, farmers argue the state marketing board is absorbing margins that should flow to growers. Daily Graphic presents both the government's position (price stability, forward sales) and farmers' concerns in a balanced format that is characteristic of its institutional voice. → graphic.com.gh
ECOWAS summit: Ghana calls for renewed bloc solidarity amid Sahel crisis spillover ▼
As Burkina Faso, Mali and Niger drift from ECOWAS under military rule, Ghana has positioned itself as a stabilising voice within the bloc. Daily Graphic covers Mahama's regional diplomacy and Ghana's pitch to be the democratic anchor of West Africa — a role that carries real economic opportunity if it succeeds in keeping regional trade corridors open. → graphic.com.gh

▶ Right / Conservative
Daily Guide
Centre-right independent daily. Broadly aligned with NPP values — free markets, private sector growth. Now in watchdog mode against the NDC government.
NDC's 24-hour economy plan: bold promise or impossible without reliable power grid? ▼
John Mahama campaigned on a "24-hour economy" — staggered business shifts to boost productivity. Daily Guide is sceptical, pointing to Ghana's persistent power supply problems (dumsor) as the fatal flaw. The paper argues that without first solving electricity reliability, extending economic activity into evening hours simply extends the burden on an already strained grid. → dailyguideafrica.com
Opposition demands audit of NDC's first budget — "the deficit numbers don't add up" ▼
The NPP opposition has demanded an independent audit of the new government's supplementary budget, claiming the fiscal projections are optimistic and that off-balance-sheet liabilities from the previous NPP administration have been selectively included to inflate the inherited deficit. Daily Guide covers this as a legitimate accountability question — its natural role as the paper closest to the party now in opposition. → dailyguideafrica.com
Private sector voices alarm at new import levies threatening manufacturing competitiveness ▼
The Ghana Union of Traders Association and several manufacturing bodies have warned that new import levies — designed to protect local industry — risk triggering retaliatory measures and raising input costs for businesses that depend on imported raw materials. Daily Guide's pro-business editorial line gives these concerns prominent coverage, framing them as a risk to the private sector-led recovery the government has promised. → dailyguideafrica.com

🔦 Ghana's Blind Spots — What the world misses

🌊 Coastal erosion crisis: Ghana is losing up to 2 metres of coastline per year in some areas — entire fishing communities are relocating. Rarely makes international news despite being an accelerating climate emergency.
🌍 Afrocontinental migration pressure: Ghana is both a source and destination country for West African migration. The internal dynamics — economic migrants from Mali and Burkina Faso fleeing instability — are reshaping Ghana's social fabric in ways the global press entirely overlooks.
📱 Fintech leadership: Ghana is one of Africa's most advanced mobile money markets. The interoperability of its payment systems — allowing transfers across networks — is a model being studied globally, but the story rarely escapes the specialist press.

◀ Left / Progressive
Mail & Guardian
South Africa's premier investigative weekly. Progressive, anti-corruption, historically anti-apartheid. The paper the powerful fear most.
GNU fault lines: ANC and DA's budget clash reveals how fragile the unity government really is ▼
South Africa's Government of National Unity — formed after the ANC lost its majority in the May 2024 election — is showing significant strain. The Mail & Guardian reports that ANC and DA negotiators are deadlocked on social grant increases vs fiscal discipline, with the DA threatening to vote against the budget. The paper frames this as a fundamental values clash, not merely a negotiating tactic. → mg.co.za
Land reform stalled: 30 years after democracy, 72% of commercial farmland still in white ownership ▼
The Mail & Guardian's land coverage is an ongoing project — it argues that the slow pace of land redistribution is not just an economic failure but a political time bomb. With the EFF and MK Party radicalising the debate and the DA resistant to expropriation, the ANC is caught between coalition stability and its own base's expectations. The paper calls for urgent legislative progress before the issue is fully captured by extremes on both sides. → mg.co.za
State capture trials: Zondo Commission leads to convictions — but the big fish remain free ▼
South Africa's decade-long state capture scandal — in which billions were looted from state enterprises under Jacob Zuma — has produced some prosecutions but the M&G reports mounting frustration that senior figures implicated in the Zondo Commission report continue to hold public positions or avoid prosecution. The paper tracks this as an ongoing accountability deficit threatening the credibility of South Africa's democratic institutions. → mg.co.za

◀ Centre Left
Daily Maverick
Digital-first investigative outlet. Membership-funded, fiercely independent. Broke the Gupta/state capture stories. South Africa's most-read online paper.
Eskom's grid: load shedding is over — but the infrastructure crisis that caused it is not ▼
South Africa went 300+ days without load shedding in 2024 — the end of a crisis that paralysed the economy for years. But Daily Maverick's energy desk reports that Eskom's generation capacity remains fragile, with ageing coal plants running above safe maintenance levels. The paper warns that the reprieve is temporary without a decisive shift to renewables and private generation. → dailymaverick.co.za
BRICS+ and the rand: SA's geopolitical balancing act is testing investor confidence ▼
South Africa's foreign policy — maintaining ties with Russia and China while seeking Western investment — is becoming harder to sustain. Daily Maverick's analysis notes that the GNU has shifted rhetoric slightly toward the West, but structural commitments to BRICS remain unchanged. The paper explores whether SA can credibly play both sides as great power competition intensifies. → dailymaverick.co.za
Crime index: SA records 27,000 murders in 2024 — the policy debate that never quite gets resolved ▼
South Africa's murder rate — among the highest in the world at over 45 per 100,000 — is covered by Daily Maverick with a data-driven, policy-focused lens. The paper examines policing capacity, the role of illegal firearms, gang economies, and why successive governments have failed to reduce violent crime despite it being electorally important. The GNU has pledged a new integrated policing strategy but detail remains thin. → dailymaverick.co.za

▶ Right / Conservative
The Citizen
Centre-right daily with a broad popular readership. Pro-law-and-order, sceptical of ANC economic policy, broadly aligned with DA governance values.
Property rights under threat: expropriation bill must be rejected, says business lobby ▼
South Africa's Expropriation Bill — allowing state acquisition of land, in some cases without compensation — is The Citizen's most consistent source of editorial anxiety. Business groups, agricultural associations and DA coalition partners have all warned it will trigger capital flight. The Citizen covers this as an existential risk to property rights and investor confidence, and calls on the ANC to withdraw the bill as a condition of GNU stability. → citizen.co.za
Crime and policing: SAPS resource crisis leaves citizens unprotected — time for private sector solutions ▼
The South African Police Service faces a staffing and resource crisis. The Citizen argues the government must both boost SAPS funding and enable greater private security provision in a structured way. The paper is sympathetic to neighbourhood patrol schemes and community policing forums, reflecting its readership's practical experience of living with high crime in urban and suburban South Africa. → citizen.co.za
DA in the GNU: Steenhuisen insists coalition is worth it — but the ANC keeps testing patience ▼
The Citizen — broadly sympathetic to the DA — is tracking the difficult calculus facing John Steenhuisen's party inside the GNU. The paper reports that DA members in the coalition say they are influencing economic policy positively, but that the ANC's continued land reform push, appointment of cadre-deployment loyalists, and resistance to private sector electricity generation are testing the limits of the arrangement. → citizen.co.za

🔦 South Africa's Blind Spots — What the world misses

🌊 Water crisis: Johannesburg — Africa's wealthiest city — regularly runs out of water due to crumbling infrastructure and an ageing Rand Water system. This is not a drought story; it's an infrastructure collapse story that the international press consistently undercoveres.
🌍 SA as Africa's anchor democracy: South Africa's political trajectory matters far beyond its borders. If the GNU fails and ANC-populist alliances entrench, the ripple effects across Southern Africa will be significant — but the world's media covers SA as a local story, not a continental bellwether.
💡 Rooftop solar revolution: Load shedding triggered an extraordinary private solar uptake — SA added more rooftop solar per capita than almost any country in the world in 2023–24. This clean energy transformation is happening organically, from the bottom up, and is largely invisible to international energy commentators.
