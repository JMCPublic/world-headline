🚫 Why some countries aren't here

World Headline covers countries where a genuinely independent press exists — papers that can challenge the government without fear of shutdown, arrest, or forced closure. Some countries have no such press. Covering them would mean presenting state propaganda as journalism, which defeats the point. We note their absence rather than pretend they don't exist.
🇰🇵 North Korea — state monolith, no independent press 🇹🇲 Turkmenistan — one of the world's most closed media environments 🇪🇷 Eritrea — "the North Korea of Africa", zero press freedom 🇧🇾 Belarus — independent media exiled or jailed since 2020 🇷🇺 Russia — independent press effectively eliminated since 2022 🇨🇳 China — all major outlets state-controlled 🇸🇦 Saudi Arabia — no independent press permitted 🇮🇷 Iran — independent journalists routinely imprisoned
