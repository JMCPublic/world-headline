🌍 Best Place to Be

Pick your political identity — see which countries have the strongest mainstream representation for your views, ranked by how closely a real party matches your position on the spectrum.
⚠ Why some countries aren't here
The Axis of Equal can only chart countries with genuine multiparty competition and a press that operates independently of the state. Countries where the government controls political outcomes or suppresses opposition can't be honestly placed on a spectrum — their "parties" don't represent real public political positions.
🇨🇳 China — one-party state 🇷🇺 Russia — managed democracy 🇸🇦 Saudi Arabia — no parties 🇰🇵 North Korea — totalitarian 🇮🇷 Iran — theocratic control 🇧🇾 Belarus — authoritarian 🇹🇲 Turkmenistan — one-party 🇪🇷 Eritrea — no elections
How is Best Place to Be scored?
Each party on the chart is given a spectrum position from 0 to 100 — 0 is the furthest left, 100 the furthest right, 50 is the centre. These positions are editorial assessments based on manifestos, stated policies, and voting records.

When you pick a political identity, it maps to an ideal position on that same scale (e.g. Social Democrat = 38, Liberal/Centrist = 50, Traditional Conservative = 73). The tool then looks at every country and finds the party whose position is closest to your ideal.

Score formula: max(0, round(100 − distance × 2.2))
A perfect match scores 100. Each point of distance costs 2.2 points. A party 45 points away scores 1; any further and the country drops off the list entirely.

Positions are editorial assessments based on manifestos, stated positions, and voting records — not algorithmic.
